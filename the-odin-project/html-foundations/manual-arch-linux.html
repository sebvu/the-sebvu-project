Installing Arch Linux

So you're interesting in installing Arch Linux?

And you want to manually install it?

Then you've come to the right place! Screw install scripts. We're doing it manual labor

Pre-Requisites

We will be dual booting on our system, so here are the pre-requisites.

This blog post is assumimg you are operating on Windows 11.

  • x86_64 (i.e. 64 bit) compatible machine
  • Recommended 2 GB RAM, minimum of 512 GB
  • Recommended 20 GB disk space, minimum 800 MB
  • 8GB+ Pendrive (EVERYTHING WILL BE WIPED)

If you have all of these, lets get started!

Installing Arch Linux

Creating Partitions

Open windows search bar, and type diskmgmt to access disk manager where you can view all connected drives and their respective partitions.

From here, you want to look for your drive of choice (typically (C: D: E:, or maybe under another alias) and shrink a minimum of 20GB of space for Arch Linux.

This will create an unallocated free space. Now we have free space for installing Arch Linux!

Downloading ISO

Choose the closes mirror to your location, then download the Arch ISO here.here.

Connect your USB stick to your computer. We will be burning our ISO into our USB using rufus and creating a bootable USB drive

WARNING: THIS WILL WIPE ANY CONTENTS IN YOUR USB STICK

Open rufus, and select boot selection and look for your downloaded archlinux ISO.

Once it is selected, click start, agree to all of the options, and wait.

Once you have created your bootable USB, it is time to create a restore point.

Windows Restore Point

We are going to taking a snapshotof the current state of windows system.

In windows search bar, search up Create a restore point, click on your current drive, click on 'create a restore point' and click okay.

If anything goes wrong with the arch installation, you can reboot to that saved window state.

Once that is done, we will now be going to our UEFI settings.

UEFI Settings

Search up in your windows search bar UEFI settings and choose to reeboot to UEFI settings. You can also restart you system and spam F12 + F2 (may vary with different computers).

Navigate to your UEFI settings, enable USB boot, and set boot priorty> for USB drive. You will also have to disable secure boot option.

You can now restart your system. You will see a few bootable options, choose Arch Linux

You are now in arch linux!

Booted up Arch Linux!

You will be presented with a command line. To make it readable, increase the font size by typing setfont ter-132n
